Peter

After the disciples scattered in the garden, Peter follows at a distance. They journey to the high priest Caiaphas' house to begin Jesus' line of questioning. Peter dares to go only as far as the courtyard.

As Peter enters, the night is cold and the air carries the scent of burning wood and damp stone. Shadows flicker against the walls, cast by the restless flames of a small fire where a group of servants and soldiers have gathered. Their murmurs fill the space — talk of the trial, the Nazarene, of what would become of Him.

Peter stands among them, his hands outstretched to the fire, though no warmth could reach the chill sinking into his bones. His heart pounds as he steals glances toward the inner chambers where Jesus has been taken. He wants to be near — but not too close. He wants to be faithful — but not found.

Then, a small voice cuts through the night.

You were with Him, weren't you? That man from Galilee?

The words strike like a hammer. Eyes turn toward him. Fear seizes him.

I don't know what you're talking about.

Moments pass. Another voice, more confident:

I've seen you with Him before!

Panic flares.

No! I swear, I don't know Him!

The night closes in. The fire crackles. Then a final accusation — from a man who had seen Peter in the garden. "You are one of them! Your accent gives you away!"

And then, the words spill out — bitter and desperate:

I do not know the man!

The instant the words leave his lips, the piercing cry of a rooster splits the night — fulfilling the words Jesus had spoken at the last supper:

Truly, I tell you, this very night, before the rooster crows, you will deny me three times.

— Matthew 26:34, ESV

Peter freezes. The fire suddenly seems unbearably bright, exposing his shame, his cowardice, his failure. Slowly, he turns — and there, in the distance, Jesus looks at him.

Not with anger. Not with condemnation. With sorrow.

The weight of it shatters him. He stumbles back, past the murmuring crowd and the glow of the fire, until the shadows swallow him whole. And there, alone in the darkness, Peter weeps.

Hosanna

The sun rises over the horizon, casting a golden glow upon the bustling streets of Jerusalem. Excitement fills the air as the cries of the people echo off the stone walls.

Hosanna! Blessed is He who comes in the name of the Lord!

A crowd gathers, their faces a tapestry of hope and anticipation. Children run ahead, their laughter mingling with the shouts of the faithful. Along the path, branches are waved high, creating a canopy of green that dances in the gentle breeze.

In the midst of this vibrant throng, a humble figure rides on a young donkey, its coat shimmering under the radiant sun. Jesus gazes upon the people, His heart full of compassion. The joyous shouts rise like a melody, a chorus of expectation:

Hosanna to the Son of David!

As He passes, garments are laid before Him on the ground, a sign of reverence and honor. The aroma of fresh blooms fills the air, mingling with the dust of the road — a tapestry of scents that embody both celebration and reverence.

Yet even as the hosannas hang sweet in the air, a shadow stirs beneath the jubilation. These same voices, so full of praise today, so certain of the king they believed had come to rescue them — before the week is done, some of them will stand in another crowd, in another moment, and let a very different word rise from their lips.

Crucify Him.

The Tomb

The stone is rolled. The grave clothes lie empty.

What was placed here in sorrow did not stay. What was sealed in darkness did not remain. What the world declared finished — was only just beginning.

Come in and sit here for a moment.

This is not a place of defeat. This tomb, this hollow place carved out of rock, became the most important room in all of human history. Because what happened here changed everything.

It changed everything for you.

He knew your name before the nails were driven. He saw your face in the garden when He sweat drops of blood and chose, in anguish, to go forward anyway. Every stripe laid upon His back, every thorn pressed into His brow, every step toward Golgotha — He took it with you in mind. Not just the world in some broad, distant sense.

You. Your failures. Your shame. The things you have done and the things that have been done to you. The weight you carry that no one else can see.

He carried it first. And then He laid it in this tomb. And then He rose — and left it here.

The grave could not hold Him, because love that strong cannot be buried. Death had no claim on the One who is Life itself. On the third day, in the gray quiet of early morning, before the world was fully awake, God rewrote the ending of every human story.

He did this for me.

Not for a concept. Not for a crowd. For me. For the version of me that is hardest to love. For the moments I am most ashamed of. For the future I was certain I had ruined beyond repair.

He did this for me.

Stand here and let that settle into you. You are not here by accident. You were drawn to this place by the same love that drove Him to the cross and raised Him from the dead. The same voice that called Lazarus out of his tomb is speaking to whatever is buried inside of you right now.

Come out.

The stone has been rolled away. The grave clothes are folded. The tomb is empty.

And because it is empty — you do not have to be.

He did this for you.
